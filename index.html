Creator Trust Problem

NovaSonic’s creator ecosystem shows signs of trust pressure. Only 22% of creators remain active after six months, while the top 3% generate 81% of listens. This dashboard is designed to reduce uncertainty by explaining how visibility may work and what creators can do next.

Creator trust

Low retention suggests that many creators may not see a clear or trustworthy route to success on the platform.

Recommendation transparency

When creators cannot understand why content is promoted, ignored or receives limited reach, they may interpret the system as arbitrary.

Platform fairness

Extreme concentration of listens may create the perception that success is only available to a small creator elite.

Assumption: the case does not confirm the exact recommendation model. This prototype therefore uses cautious language and plausible visibility signals for demonstration purposes.

Recommendation Transparency

Creators need understandable explanations. These example cards show how NovaSonic could explain visibility without overclaiming certainty.

This episode may be recommended because recent listeners completed most of it and engagement signals suggest that the topic is relevant to similar audiences.
This episode may receive less exposure because early listener drop-off is high or because its topic signals are too broad to match a specific audience.
This episode may be recommended to a narrow audience because its content appears niche or strongly linked to a specific listener segment.
Metadata may help the platform understand what the episode is about. Clear titles, descriptions and categories can support better matching with relevant listeners.

Platform Fairness and Concentration Risk

When 3% of creators generate 81% of listens, creators outside that group may perceive the platform as difficult to break into. A transparency dashboard should therefore show not only performance, but also how visibility opportunities are distributed.

Data-use Transparency

If NovaSonic uses AI or algorithmic recommendation systems, creators should understand what kinds of data may influence visibility and how governance is applied.

What data may be used?

  • Listening behaviour
  • Episode metadata
  • Search and discovery activity
  • Engagement signals
  • Creator publishing patterns

What should NovaSonic explain?

  • Whether recommendations are AI-based, rules-based, editorial or hybrid
  • What data influences visibility
  • Whether creators can appeal or query visibility decisions
  • Whether fairness and concentration are audited
  • How creators can access their own performance data

Creator Action Plan

The dashboard should give creators practical actions rather than simply showing performance data.

Improve episode metadata.

Use clear titles, descriptions and categories so the platform can better understand the episode.

Publish consistently for four weeks.

Consistent publishing may create stronger audience and platform signals.

Track completion rate.

Episodes that retain listener attention may be easier to recommend to similar audiences.

Review audience drop-off points.

Drop-off patterns can help creators improve episode structure and pacing.

Encourage follows and saves.

These engagement signals may indicate audience value and support discoverability.

Compare performance by topic and format.

This helps creators identify which formats build stronger audience trust and retention.

Trust Commitments

To rebuild creator confidence, NovaSonic should treat transparency as a governance commitment, not simply an analytics feature.

Explain recommendation factors

Provide plain-language explanations of the signals that may affect visibility.

Communicate data use

Tell creators what data is used and how it supports recommendation or discovery decisions.

Monitor fairness

Review whether the top 3% listen concentration is worsening or improving over time.

Support creator queries

Allow creators to query major visibility changes or unexplained performance drops.

Final Business Recommendation

NovaSonic should pilot this Creator Transparency Dashboard with new and mid-tier creators, because these creators are most likely to experience uncertainty around visibility. Success should be measured through creator trust scores, dashboard engagement, creator retention after six months, and changes in the distribution of listens across creator tiers.
